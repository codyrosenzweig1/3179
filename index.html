Energy Generation & Consumption

1988-2021 by Cody Rosenzweig in FIT3179

Energy has played a pivotal role throughout human history, from early harnessing of fire for warmth and cooking to the industrial revolution's reliance on steam power. It remains an essential driving force in modern civilization, powering everything from our daily lives to advancements in technology, industry, and sustainable development, highlighting its enduring significance in shaping our world.

So, why the Mediterranean?

As the melting pot of the Worlds cultures, religions and history, the Meditteranean seems like a reasonable spot to begin our investigation into humankinds' Energy production and consumption trends and habits.



Is there a difference between surrounding regions?

Each of the regions within the general area of the Mediterranean share an energy profile which is usually dominated by one or two countries. Indeed, these countries such as Russia, Iran and Egypt all have large populations indicating a logical idea that in order to function these countries must consume and produce large sums of energy! We should investigate this theory!

Is there a relationship between Population and Energy consumption?

There appears to be a clear relationship between Population and Energy consumption! As time progresses it is clear that all data points are shiting upwards, suggesting that as humanity continues to develop our need for energy will continue to grow. But does this relationship translate on a global scale?




Does this pattern exist worldwide?

Bigger Generators and Consumers outside of Asia

As so clearly evident in the worldwide map above, almost all the major players in the energy industry exist outside of the Mediterranean. This makes sense due to the extreme populations of some Asian countries.

It is facinating to see how our trend follows suit on a global scale with the more densley populated region of Asia dominating both energy conumption and generation over time. The worldwide trend seems to exaggerate the accelerated growth we saw earlier.

The rapid growth of China's generation is indicative of their hightened industrialisation. China's enourmous population combined with its place as the world's largest producer of goods and materials has paved the way for its rapid domestic energy generation plans



As perhaps the most convincing argument of the global need of continued energy generation is the bubble plot above highlighting the clear movement of all regions and population sizes towards both greater energy consumption and generation.



Concluding notes

humankinds needs for energy have always existed and will continue to exist and grow for centuries to come. As a result of growing populations and innovative technologies, energy consumption and production are likely to exist as long as humanity does!!
